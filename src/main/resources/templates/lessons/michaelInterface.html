A set of classes is used to represent various items that are available for purchase. Items are either taxable or nontaxable. The purchase price of a taxable item is computed from its list price and its tax rate. The purchase price of a nontaxable item is simply its list price. Part of the class hierarchy is shown in the diagram below.

The definitions of the Item interface and the Taxable Item class are shown below.

                            // returns the price of the item including the tax

For example, if the tax rate is 0.10 (representing 10%), the purchase price of an item with a list price of $6.50 would be $7.15.

  2. Create the Vehicle, which extends the TaxableItem class. A vehicle has two parts to its list price: a dealer cost and a dealer markup. The list price of the vehicle is the sum of the dealer cost and the dealer markup.
    For example, if a vehicle has a dealer cost of $20,000.00, a deal markup of $2,500.00, and a tax rate of 0.10, then the list price of the vehicle would be $22,500.00 and the purchase price (including tax) would be $24,750.00. If the dealer markup were changed to $1,000.00, then the list price of the vehicle would be $21,000.00 and the purchase price would be $23,100.00.
    Your class should have a constructor that takes dealer cost, the dealer markup, and the tax rate as parameters. Provide any private instance variables needed and implement all necessary methods. Also provide a public method changeMarkup, which changes the dealer markup to the value of its parameter.
Interface vs. Abstract Classes

Interfaces consist of many methods with no implementations that can be implemented into another class. These classes also do not have instance variables, unless they are statics.

An abstract class can have instance variables as well as abstract methods (unimplemented) and implemented methods.

Why is getListPrice() abstract?
In the class hierarchy above, a child of TaxableItem appears to be a vehicle. The vehicle use is just an example of a product and since many different products have different list prices, the getListPrice method is abstract. The list price can now be obtained from the child class (in this case Vehicle).
